Napolitin Spaghetti

A mix of Eastern and Western Cuisine

A Japanese-style pasta dish made with a sauce that is tomato ketchup-based. Usually includes bell pepper, mushrooms, onions, sausage, and bacon. It is a fairly simple dish that is great for beginners. I honestly chose it bacuase I like to use ketchup as a sauce for leftover spaghetti.

I got this recipe off of another website which I will include a link here. All credits to Namiko Chen of Just One Cookbook. There is way more information about the recipe and some indredients that were optional that I didn't include. I also removed and halved ingredients, but I will include what I adjusted in the notes.

Ingredients

  • 7 oz spaghetti (I used Angel Hair Pasta for mine)
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 1/4 onion
  • 2 Italian Sausages
  • 6 mushrooms
  • 2 Tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 Tbsp milk

Sauce

  • 4 Tbsp Ketchup
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1-3 Tbsp reserved pasta water
  • salt
  • freshly ground black pepper

Instructions

  1. Gather all the ingredients you want to use.
  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the pasta.
  3. While its cooking, mince the garlic clove and slice onion, sausages, bell pepper, and mushrooms.
  4. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté for 1 minute, just until golden and fragrant.
  5. Add the onions to the pan and sauté for 2-3 minutes or until wilted.
  6. Add the sausages and sauté for 1 minute
  7. Add the mushrooms and sauté until everything is cooked.

My Experience

The food turned out great for my first time though I went over estimated time. It took me closer to an hour because it took me a while to prep the ingredients. I was also scrambling during the cooking process. I had to measure the ingredients for the sauce because I didn't measure them before hand.

I adjusted the ingredients depending on what I had at the time as well as to fit my family's preference. I removed green pepper and halved the onions. Not a big fan of vegetables and neither are my sisters. I also used some lactose milk because that is the only milk we had. Still tasted good so I don't think it affected the result. There was no cheese in the house so I wasn't able to add it, but I will be trying it next time. I did however add some mozerella cheese after the fact and it tasted pretty good. That might be more a preference though.

All in all, it is a great recipe. It was fairly easy.
